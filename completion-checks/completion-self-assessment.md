### Evaluate

*The ability to define success before building, measure output quality against defined criteria, and iterate with documented evidence — across multiple models.*

The skills I have developed going through the course content and exercises have given me the confidence to apply these principles in my real work. Over time, my understanding of Promptfoo and incrementally developing "good practices" has grown, and I now feel confident in my ability to use it effectively.

I have also started to reach out to other Improvers to share my experiences and learn from theirs.

Although I possess a working knowledge of evaluation, I still need to practice applying it more systematically in my real work. As I do this more frequently, I will be better at:

I am getting better, but identifying what stays and what goes when making changes to produce better results takes continuous practice.

### Iterate

*The ability to diagnose why a prompt is failing — distinguishing Context problems from Constraints problems, and downstream quality problems from upstream quality problems — and to make targeted, one-change-at-a-time improvements.*

This was a challenge for me early on, but as I gained more experience with Promptfoo and RTCC, I became more confident in my ability to diagnose and trace quality problems to their source. I found ways to speed up test runs, pinpoint issues, make targeted improvements, and repeat the process until I achieved the desired results.

By the end of the course material, I felt I was in a good place to better iterate and more quickly get to the results I want. This will only get better as I am equipped to apply this knowledge in my real work.
